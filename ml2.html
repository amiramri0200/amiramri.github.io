The objective of this project is to implement a Convolutional Autoencoder (CAE) for addressing unsupervised problems. Autoencoders, in general, aim to learn a lower-dimensional representation (encoding) for higher-dimensional data, primarily for dimensionality reduction. The training process involves instructing the network to capture the most crucial aspects of the input image. Autoencoders also find applications in denoising and anomaly detection. In the initial phase of this assignment, we define a Convolutional Autoencoder and train it using various optimizers to assess their performance on the Fashion MNIST dataset. Following this, utilizing the decoder component of the Autoencoder, we compute the latent space for the training set and present a visualization in two-dimensional space. Subsequently, we introduce a Variational Autoencoder (VAE), train it, and visualize the latent space in both 2D and 3D, comparing it with the conventional CAE. The exploration extends to assessing the impact of Principal Component Analysis (PCA) and t-Distributed Stochastic Neighbor Embedding (t-SNE) on the autoencoder with a higher-dimensional latent space. Finally, we delve into the creation of a Denoising Autoencoder (DAE). This involves feeding the network with noisy inputs to investigate the performance of DAEs in the presence of noise.
